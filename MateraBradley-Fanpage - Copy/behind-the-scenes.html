Set Design and Locations

Explore the intricacies of the set design and the real-life filming locations that brought the world of 'Parks and Recreation' to life. Discover the stories behind these iconic locales.

The Heart of Pawnee: City Hall

The Pawnee City Hall was the central hub for many of the show's pivotal scenes. The set was designed to reflect the town's quirky charm and the Parks Department's place within it.

Real-World Locations

While Pawnee is a fictional town, many of the show's locations were filmed in real places. These locations added authenticity to the world of 'Parks and Recreation'.

Pasadena City Hall as Pawnee City Hall

Pasadena City Hall in California doubled as Pawnee's city hall, providing an elegant and grand facade that became synonymous with the series.

The Pit

The famous pit that started it all was located in a real park. This location was key to the first season's storyline, representing Leslie's passion for improving her community.

JJ's Diner

Leslie's favorite diner, known for its waffles, was filmed in a real diner. This cozy location provided a comforting backdrop for many of the show's heartwarming scenes.
